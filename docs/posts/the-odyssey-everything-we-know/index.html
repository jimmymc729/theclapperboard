Everything We Know About The Odyssey, Starring Tom Holland And Zendaya

Christopher Nolan's Homer adaptation just had its world premiere in London — here's what critics saw, and what everyone's about to see this week.

1

It's Christopher Nolan's take on Homer's Odyssey — releasing July 17, 2026

Nolan's film adapts Homer's epic poem, following Odysseus's long, monster-and-god-filled journey home after the Trojan War while his wife Penelope and son Telemachus try to hold his kingdom of Ithaca together in his absence.

2

Tom Holland plays Telemachus, Odysseus's son

Holland plays the prince of Ithaca, who spends the film trying to hold his father's kingdom together — and fend off suitors circling his mother — while searching for word of Odysseus, who's been gone for a decade.

3

Zendaya plays Athena, the goddess of wisdom and war

Zendaya takes on a mythological role rather than a mortal one, playing Athena — the goddess who protects and guides Odysseus throughout his journey. It's her first role in a Nolan film.

4

The rest of the cast is stacked

Matt Damon plays Odysseus and Anne Hathaway plays Penelope. Robert Pattinson plays Antinous, a rival vying for Penelope's hand; Charlize Theron plays the sea witch Calypso; Lupita Nyong'o plays both Helen of Troy and her sister Clytemnestra; and Jon Bernthal and Benny Safdie play the Spartan king Menelaus and his brother Agamemnon.

5

It's the first feature ever shot entirely on IMAX film cameras

Nolan shot the entire film on IMAX cameras, a first for a feature-length movie, continuing his long-running commitment to shooting on film rather than digital. Cast members have described the format's massive resolution and physical scale as central to how the movie was performed and staged.

6

Early reactions out of its London premiere call it Nolan's biggest film yet

After the review embargo lifted following the film's star-studded London premiere, early reactions were largely glowing — Variety called it "an astonishing achievement" and "a triumphant, spectacular epic," with particular praise for the Cyclops and Circe sequences and for performances from Holland, Damon, Hathaway, and Pattinson.

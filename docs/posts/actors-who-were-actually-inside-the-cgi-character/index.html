You've Been Watching Real Actors This Whole Time — You Just Didn't Know It

Behind every unforgettable CGI creature is a flesh-and-blood performance the Oscars still can't quite figure out how to reward.

1

Andy Serkis Basically Invented This Whole Category — And The Academy Still Won't Give Him An Oscar

Serkis spent years in a marker-covered mocap suit shooting Gollum's scenes alongside his co-stars, with Weta Digital translating every twitch into the final creature — and he later did the same for Caesar across the Planet of the Apes trilogy. Despite widespread campaigns, he's never landed an Oscar nomination, a snub that keeps reigniting the debate over whether performance capture counts as 'real' acting.

2

Zoe Saldana Has Given James Cameron Some Of His Best Performances — In A Blue Bodysuit Covered In Dots

To become Neytiri, Saldaña put on a marker-covered unitard and stepped into what the cast calls 'the volume' — a rigged soundstage ringed with cameras that feed every movement straight into Pandora. Cameron himself has said there's nothing about her work that's 'of a caliber less' than an Oscar winner's, and Saldaña has openly called the Academy's continued silence on mocap acting 'quite deflating.'

3

Josh Brolin's Face Is Doing More Work In Infinity War Than You Realize

Before shooting even began, Brolin sat for a high-resolution facial scan so a proprietary Digital Domain system called Masquerade could learn exactly how his face moved, then track it via a helmet camera on set. VFX supervisor Kelly Port said the whole point was capturing 'the subtleties of Josh Brolin's performances' so closely that Thanos's eyes and expressions read as genuinely human, not animated.

4

Doug Jones Has Played More Monsters Than You've Had Hot Dinners — And He's Barely Ever Shown His Own Face

Jones spent five hours in the makeup chair to become the Faun in Pan's Labyrinth, six hours for the Pale Man, and three hours a day getting zipped and glued into the Amphibian Man suit for The Shape of Water — all nearly 100% practical prosthetics rather than digital CGI. He's never spoken a word as the Shape of Water creature, telling one interviewer that in that silent romance, 'words can lie, words can deceive, but touch doesn't.'

5

Bill Nighy Played Davy Jones In A Grey Onesie He Called 'Pirate Pajamas' — And Somehow It Worked

With no rubber suit or tentacle prosthetics to hide behind, Nighy performed every scene in a plain grey motion-capture suit dotted with reference markers, describing it as playing 'the organ with an imaginary octopus beard.' The illusion landed so well that critics and even VFX insiders assumed there was a physical costume involved, when it was entirely Nighy's face and movement translated by ILM.

6

Mark Ruffalo Was The First Actor To Actually Play The Hulk — Not Just Voice Him

Ruffalo wore a triangle-patterned mocap suit and a helmet-mounted facial camera on set, letting ILM retarget his real performance onto the Hulk instead of building the character from scratch in CGI like earlier films did. He's said the goal was to make sure the Hulk looked 'just like me' — just a lot bigger — and later described how advances in the tech let him finally act scenes directly opposite his co-stars instead of alone in a mocap volume.

5 Insane Behind-The-Scenes Facts About Iconic Movies

The mechanical shark barely worked. The dinosaur cried real rainwater. Somehow, they all became classics.

1

Jaws' shark barely worked — so Spielberg just stopped showing it

The three full-size mechanical sharks built for Jaws (all nicknamed "Bruce") constantly broke down in salt water, blowing the budget from $4 million to $8 million and the shoot from 55 days to 159. Spielberg leaned on John Williams' two-note score and pure suggestion instead of showing the shark — and it became the scarier choice.

2

The Matrix's bullet time used 120 still cameras firing in a circle

That frozen-in-midair camera spin wasn't CGI trickery — it was around 120 still cameras arranged in an arc, firing in rapid sequence to fake a slow-motion orbit around a single frozen moment. The effect took months of prep and helped make an entire visual-effects term ("bullet time") mainstream.

3

Parasite was almost a stage play

Director Bong Joon-ho originally conceived Parasite as a play before realizing mid-development it needed to be a film. It went on to become the first South Korean film to win the Palme d'Or at Cannes — by unanimous jury vote — and later the first non-English-language Best Picture winner at the Oscars.

4

Jurassic Park's dinosaurs got barely 15 minutes of screen time — and only 5 were CGI

Most of the T-Rex's screen time came from a 7-ton, 40-foot animatronic built by Stan Winston's team. During the rain-drenched attack scene, the foam latex skin soaked up so much water the animatronic shuddered uncontrollably — crew had to hand-dry it with towels and blow dryers between takes.

5

Titanic's budget ran $100 million over — so James Cameron gave up his own salary

At roughly $200 million, Titanic became the most expensive film ever made at the time — and ran about $100 million over its original budget. To keep the studio calm, James Cameron forfeited his own $8 million salary and profit share. Even the "I'm the king of the world!" line wasn't scripted — Cameron told DiCaprio to improvise something on the spot.

Sharon Stone's Career Has More Twists Than A Basic Instinct Interrogation Scene

From a four-day modeling signing to an Oscar snub she still isn't over, here's the wild ride nobody fully remembers.

1

She Signed With Ford Models In Four Days Flat — Then Ditched It For A Two-Minute Movie Cameo

Sharon Stone didn't grow up dreaming of Hollywood — she was a working Ford model in New York, Milan, and Paris before she ever stepped in front of a film camera. Her actual acting debut was a blink-and-you-miss-it cameo in Woody Allen's Stardust Memories, and it barely counts as a role at all.

2

Total Recall Was Supposed To Be A Throwaway Sci-Fi Gig — It Rewrote Her Entire Career Instead

Stone played Arnold Schwarzenegger's treacherous wife in Total Recall in 1990, and the movie's massive box office success gave her her very first real taste of stardom. Director Paul Verhoeven later admitted he'd initially written her off as damaged goods from earlier bad roles, but Total Recall convinced him she had way more range than anyone was giving her credit for.

3

Casino Gave Her The Only Oscar Nomination Of Her Career — And She Still Has Feelings About Losing

As Ginger McKenna in Martin Scorsese's Casino, Stone earned her sole Academy Award nomination for Best Actress and won a Golden Globe for the role, going toe-to-toe with Robert De Niro in one of the most acclaimed performances of her career. She ultimately lost the Oscar to Susan Sarandon for Dead Man Walking, and De Niro reportedly warned her going in that she wouldn't win, telling her flatly, "You're not going to win the Oscar, Sharon."

4

One Casino Scene Made Her Genuinely Furious At Robert De Niro Mid-Take

Stone has said that during a heated argument scene with De Niro, he complimented her acting skills mid-take in a way that set off real, unscripted anger in her — she recalled thinking, "Oh, you crossed a line today, mister." She's since called landing the role opposite De Niro her longtime dream, one she'd been chasing through years of auditions before Casino finally happened.

6

A Character Was Literally Named After Her In The Flintstones — And She Turned It Down

The 1994 live-action Flintstones movie wanted the real Sharon Stone to play the flirtatious secretary character, who was even named "Miss Sharon Stone" in her honor, but she passed because she was already committed to filming The Specialist. Halle Berry ended up taking the role instead, making for one of the stranger almost-castings in '90s movie history.

7

Decades Into Her Movie Career, She Says Modeling Still Pays Better

Even with a filmography stacked with iconic roles, Stone has said she still makes more money modeling today than she does acting, calling it a huge part of her ongoing reality. She's also noted she's one of the oldest women still consistently working as a model — proof that the pivot she made as a teenager in New York never really ended.

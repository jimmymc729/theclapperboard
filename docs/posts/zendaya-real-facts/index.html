8 Facts About Zendaya's Rise To Hollywood's Most In-Demand Star

From backup dancer to the youngest two-time Emmy winner in history — and a stylist partnership that rewrote red carpet rules.

1

Her name means "to give thanks" — and she almost didn't get her breakout role

Born Zendaya Maree Coleman, her first name comes from a Shona word meaning "to give thanks." She started out as a child model and backup dancer before auditioning for Disney's Shake It Up in 2009 — originally for the lead role of CeCe, which went to Bella Thorne. Zendaya was cast as Rocky Blue instead.

2

At 16, she gave Disney a list of demands before signing her next show

Before agreeing to star in K.C. Undercover, a 16-year-old Zendaya reportedly pushed Disney on multiple points, including getting a producer credit and creative input on the show itself — unusually assertive terms for a teenager negotiating with a major studio.

3

She became the youngest-ever winner of the Lead Actress Drama Emmy — then broke her own record

Zendaya won the Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series Emmy for Euphoria in 2020 at age 24, making her the youngest winner in the category's history. In 2022, at 26, she won it again — becoming the youngest two-time winner ever, and the first Black woman to win the award twice.

4

She's also a producer, not just an actress

Zendaya co-owns a production company, Somewhere Pictures, and has an executive producer credit on Euphoria — a role that made her one of the youngest women ever recognized in that category at the Emmys, on top of her acting nominations.

5

Her decade-long partnership with stylist Law Roach pioneered 'method styling'

Zendaya was Law Roach's first major client when he moved to Los Angeles to become a stylist. Together they built a reputation for "method styling" — red carpet looks that visually echo whatever film she's promoting, most famously the sci-fi-inspired gowns during the Dune: Part Two press tour and sport-influenced looks for Challengers.

6

For The Odyssey's premiere, her gown was flown from a Paris runway reveal straight to the red carpet

A Schiaparelli Haute Couture gown was unveiled on the runway in Paris and, the same day, arranged for private-jet transport to London so Zendaya could wear it to The Odyssey's premiere — a logistical stunt that became its own viral fashion moment.

5 Actors Who Transformed Their Bodies For A Role — And What It Actually Cost Them

Some won Oscars for it. A couple of them also ended up in a doctor's office.

1

Christian Bale dropped 63 pounds for The Machinist — and producers had to step in

To play an insomniac factory worker wasting away with guilt, Bale dropped from a healthy 173 pounds to about 110 over roughly four months. He reportedly wanted to lose even more, but filmmakers and doctors intervened out of concern for his health. He gained the weight back — and then bulked up again for Batman Begins the very same year.

2

Matthew McConaughey lost 47 pounds for Dallas Buyers Club — and won the Oscar

Playing Ron Woodroof, a real man diagnosed with AIDS in the 1980s, McConaughey went from 183 pounds to 136 over about six months under close supervision. The transformation, paired with his performance, won him the Academy Award for Best Actor.

3

Charlize Theron gained 30 pounds to play a real serial killer in Monster

To play Aileen Wuornos, Theron gained roughly 30 pounds and altered her appearance with prosthetic teeth and makeup, saying she needed to physically feel different in order to access the character honestly. It won her the Academy Award for Best Actress.

4

Jared Leto gained 67 pounds for Chapter 27 — and developed gout because of it

To play John Lennon's killer, Mark David Chapman, Leto gained 67 pounds in a short window. The rapid weight gain led to gout, elevated cholesterol, and back pain severe enough that he reportedly used a wheelchair for a period during production — a transformation he's since described in interviews as far more physically damaging than he expected.

5

Chris Hemsworth gained 20 pounds of muscle for the original Thor — the healthier version of this story

Before playing the God of Thunder, Hemsworth spent about six months on a supervised strength-training program and a high-calorie diet with a trainer, adding roughly 20 pounds of muscle. Unlike several entries on this list, it's widely cited as a relatively sustainable transformation — built through consistent training rather than extreme deprivation.

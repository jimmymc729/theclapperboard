“Every one of my shots is composed with near-mathematical symmetry — my crew has measured frames to keep the camera dead center. I lean on pastel color palettes and actors who deliver even the strangest lines completely deadpan.”
Reveal the answer

Answer

Wes Anderson

2
“I appeared in a brief cameo in nearly every one of the more than 50 films I directed — often carrying a prop like a musical instrument, or just walking through the background. Spotting me became a game for audiences.”
Reveal the answer

Answer

Alfred Hitchcock

3
“I almost never tell a story in chronological order. I've got a signature low camera angle shot from inside a car trunk looking up, and I have a well-documented habit of lingering on close-ups of bare feet.”
Reveal the answer

Answer

Quentin Tarantino

4
“My films tend to look gothic and slightly crooked, like the buildings themselves are in mourning. I've cast the same actor in eight of my movies, and almost every score I've ever used comes from the same longtime composer.”
Reveal the answer

Answer

Tim Burton

5
“I'd rather blow up a real building than render one on a computer — my films use a fraction of the visual-effects shots of a typical blockbuster. I also have a reputation for telling stories out of chronological order, sometimes even in reverse.”
Reveal the answer

Answer

Christopher Nolan

6
“Almost every movie I've directed ends with a twist nobody sees coming — and I've written myself a small on-screen role into nearly all of them, too.”

Answer

M. Night Shyamalan

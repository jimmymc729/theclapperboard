These 5 Movies Spent Almost As Much Convincing You To See Them As They Did Making Them

Turns out the budget on the poster is only half the story — here's what studios ACTUALLY spent once marketing got involved.

1

Green Lantern Spent More Hyping Itself Than Some Movies Cost To Make, Period

Warner Bros. handed Ryan Reynolds a $200 million production budget for this DC origin story, then reportedly piled on close to $100-125 million more just to market it worldwide. The film limped to $219.9 million globally, and Warner Bros. quietly shelved its entire planned franchise.

2

John Carter's Marketing Was SO Bad It Became Part Of Its Own Flop Story

Disney spent an already-eye-watering $250 million making this Edgar Rice Burroughs adaptation, then added another $100 million trying to get anyone to understand what it was even about. The confusing ad campaign is widely blamed for the movie's collapse, and Disney booked a historic $200 million write-down.

3

The Lone Ranger's Ad Budget Alone Could've Funded An Entire Other Movie

Disney's Johnny Depp western ran up a production tab north of $215-250 million, then layered on a reported $150 million marketing blitz on top of that. It grossed $260.5 million worldwide — nowhere close to covering both numbers — and the studio ate a write-down as high as $190 million.

4

Snow White and the Huntsman Nearly Doubled Its Budget Just In Ads

Universal's dark fairy-tale reboot cost a reported $170 million to produce, and industry estimates put the marketing and distribution spend at another $80-90 million on top of that. It actually did clear its production budget at the box office — but factoring in the ad spend made real profit a much murkier picture.

5

Justice League Needed $450 Million Just To Break Even — And Still Didn't

Between its reported $300 million production budget and an estimated $150 million global marketing spend, Warner Bros. needed the superhero team-up to gross around $450-750 million just to come out even. It made $657.9 million worldwide and was STILL considered a financial disappointment.

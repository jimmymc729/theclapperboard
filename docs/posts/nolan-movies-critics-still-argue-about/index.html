Nolan's Having A Monster Moment Right Now — But These 5 Movies Still Split Everyone In Half

While critics fall over themselves for 'The Odyssey,' let's not pretend Christopher Nolan's filmography is one long unbroken victory lap — these five movies still start arguments.

1

Tenet Got The Reviews... And Then Audiences Handed Nolan His Lowest CinemaScore In 14 Years

Tenet is the reminder that Nolan's audience adoration isn't unconditional. Critics were split down the middle between calling it a masterpiece and calling it exhausting, and regular moviegoers backed that up with a shockingly average grade.

2

Interstellar's 'Love Transcends Dimensions' Speech Still Splits The Room A Decade Later

Interstellar earned five Oscar nominations and a reputation as one of Nolan's most emotional films, but that same tesseract-and-bookshelf finale — built around the idea that love itself might be a fifth-dimensional force — has been called everything from profound to a cop-out. It tied Tenet for years as Nolan's lowest-rated film on Rotten Tomatoes, proof the ending really did cost it critics.

3

The Dark Knight Rises Made $1 Billion — And Still Gets Dragged For Its Plot Logic

Batman's finale was a box office behemoth, but even fans who love the trilogy admit the third act asks a lot of your patience: a five-month occupation of Gotham, a miraculously escaped Bruce Wayne with no money, and a nuke somehow detonated safely at the last possible second. Writers have called it flatly the weakest entry in the trilogy on pure plot mechanics, even while praising its operatic ambition.

4

Dunkirk Scored A Best Picture Nomination With Zero Actors Nominated Anywhere — That's Not Nothing

Dunkirk pulled eight Oscar nominations, but not a single one went to an actor, a rarity that fueled real debate about whether the film's deliberately thin characterization — nobody in the ensemble even gets a proper introduction — was bold minimalism or a genuine weakness. It ultimately lost Best Picture to The Shape of Water without ever cracking the acting or screenplay branches.

5

Inception Is Beloved, Sure — But 'Style Over Coherence' Talk Has Followed It For 15 Years

Inception is one of Nolan's most acclaimed films, yet the backlash-to-the-backlash never fully went away: plenty of viewers over the years have dismissed it as all flash and no substance, a puzzle box that prioritizes cool visuals over an emotionally graspable story. Even its most iconic beat, that spinning top, has spent a decade and a half being called either endlessly rewatchable or wildly overhyped, depending who you ask.

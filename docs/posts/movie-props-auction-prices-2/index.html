5 More Movie Props That Sold For Jaw-Dropping Amounts At Auction

A car. A blaster. Two dresses. One door. Here's what they all actually sold for.

1

James Bond's Aston Martin DB5 sold for $6.4 million

One of the original gadget-equipped Aston Martin DB5s built for the James Bond films — complete with revolving license plates, machine guns, and an ejector seat — sold at RM Sotheby's 2019 Monterey auction for $6,385,000. It's widely considered the most valuable movie car ever sold at auction.

2

Han Solo's blaster from Star Wars sold for $1,057,500

The only surviving screen-used BlasTech DL-44 blaster from Star Wars: A New Hope — Harrison Ford's Han Solo prop — sold at Rock Island Auction Company in 2022, setting the Guinness World Record for the most expensive prop gun ever sold at auction.

3

Marilyn Monroe's 'subway' dress from The Seven Year Itch sold for $5.66 million

The white halter dress Marilyn Monroe wore over a subway grate in The Seven Year Itch sold for $4.6 million at hammer — $5,658,000 with buyer's premium — at a 2011 Profiles in History auction of Debbie Reynolds' Hollywood costume collection, more than double its $2 million estimate.

4

Audrey Hepburn's little black dress from Breakfast at Tiffany's sold for $923,187

The black Givenchy gown Audrey Hepburn wore in the opening shot of Breakfast at Tiffany's sold at a 2006 Christie's auction in London for £467,200 ($923,187) — more than six times its £70,000 estimate. Proceeds went to a children's education charity in Kolkata.

5

The 'door' from Titanic sold for $718,750

The wooden panel Rose floats on at the end of Titanic — technically part of a first-class lounge door frame, not a door — sold for $718,750 at a 2024 Heritage Auctions sale, after opening bids of $40,000 spiraled into a bidding war within minutes.
